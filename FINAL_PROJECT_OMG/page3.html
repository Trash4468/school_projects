Nirvana's Popular Albums

Nevermind

This album catapulted Nirvana to global fame. With hits like "Smells Like Teen Spirit," it defined the grunge movement of the '90s. The raw energy, catchy riffs, and Kurt Cobain's unique voice resonated with a generation.

In Utero

Known for its more abrasive and unpolished sound compared to Nevermind, In Utero was critically acclaimed for its rawness and honesty. Songs like "Heart-Shaped Box" and "All Apologies" showcased Cobain's songwriting at its best.

Bleach

Nirvana's debut album, released on the indie label Sub Pop, captured the attention of the underground music scene. It laid the groundwork for their future success, with its gritty sound and tracks like "About a Girl."
